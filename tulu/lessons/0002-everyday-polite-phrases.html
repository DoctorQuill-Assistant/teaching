Everyday polite phrases

In this lesson, you will learn short, high-frequency Tulu phrases for being polite in everyday conversation: saying thank you, asking for help, saying yes or no, and speaking gently when you do not understand.

Focus: short spoken phrases you can start using right away.

What success looks like

  • Say thank you naturally.
  • Ask for help politely.
  • Say yes and no.
  • Say you do not know or do not understand.

Core phrases

Mast upakāra. = Thank you very much.
Daye malpule. = Please help.
Avu. = Okay / yes / alright.
Ijji. = No / not.
Yān Tulu barpuji. = I do not know Tulu.
Enk artha apuji. = I do not understand.
Bokka oripule. = Please say it again. (learner-support phrase; practical approximation based on source patterns)
Dāye, nidāna pander. = Please speak slowly. (learner-support phrase; practical approximation based on source patterns)

How to use them

Polite response

  • Mast upakāra. — strong thank you
  • Avu. — okay, yes, alright
  • Ijji. — no

When you need support

  • Daye malpule. — please help
  • Enk artha apuji. — I do not understand
  • Bokka oripule. — please say it again

Useful words

  • mast — very / a lot
  • upakāra — thanks / favor / help
  • daye — kindness / please
  • artha — meaning / understanding
  • apuji — not possible / does not happen / cannot
  • bokka — again / then / and, depending on context
  • nidāna — slowly

Mini dialogue: asking for help

You: Namaskāra.

You: Yān Tulu barpuji.

You: Daye malpule.

Other person: Avu.

You: Mast upakāra.

Mini dialogue: you did not catch it

Other person: ...

You: Enk artha apuji.

You: Bokka oripule.

You: Dāye, nidāna pander.

Other person: Avu.

Pronunciation confidence hints

  • mast upakāra ≈ "muhst oo-puh-KAA-ruh"
  • daye malpule ≈ "DAH-yeh mahl-poo-leh"
  • avu ≈ "AH-voo"
  • ijji ≈ "IJ-jee"
  • artha apuji ≈ "AR-thuh ah-poo-jee"
  • bokka oripule ≈ "BOK-kuh oh-ri-poo-leh"
  • nidāna pander ≈ "nih-DAA-nuh pahn-DER"

These are learner-friendly approximations for confidence, not exact phonetics.

Small cultural note

When your Tulu is still beginner-level, simple honesty sounds polite: say you do not know Tulu, ask for help, and ask the speaker to repeat slowly.

That keeps the conversation going even with a very small vocabulary.
