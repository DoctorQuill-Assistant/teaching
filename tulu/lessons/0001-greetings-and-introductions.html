Tulu • Lesson 1

Greetings and introductions

In this lesson, you will learn a small set of practical beginner Tulu phrases for greeting people, introducing yourself, asking someone’s name, and responding politely.

Focus: spoken confidence first, with learner-friendly pronunciation hints.

Ask follow-up pronunciation questions any time. Tulu has limited learning resources, so each lesson will stay practical and carefully sourced.

What success looks like

  • Greet someone politely.
  • Say “My name is …”
  • Ask “What is your name?”
  • Say “I am fine.”

The key beginner patterns

Namaskāra. = Hello / Greetings.
Ninna pudar enchina? = What is your name?
Enna pudar … = My name is …
Yān kusald aande. = I am fine.

These are strong starter phrases for first conversations.

Core phrases

Namaskāra. = Hello.
Yān kusald aande. = I am fine.
Ninna pudar enchina? = What is your name?
Enna pudar Maya. = My name is Maya.
I ench ullar? = How are you?
Yān Tulu barpuji. = I do not know Tulu.
Daye malpule. = Please help.
Mast upakāra. = Thank you very much.

Useful words

  • yān — I
  • ninna — your
  • enna — my
  • pudar — name
  • enchina — what?
  • kusald — well / fine
  • upakāra — thanks / help given
  • daye — kindness / please

Mini dialogue: first meeting

Person A: Namaskāra.

Person B: Namaskāra.

Person A: Ninna pudar enchina?

Person B: Enna pudar Ravi.

Person B: I ench ullar?

Person A: Yān kusald aande.

Mini dialogue: beginner honesty

You: Namaskāra.

You: Enna pudar Alex.

You: Yān Tulu barpuji.

You: Daye malpule.

Pronunciation confidence hints

  • Namaskāra ≈ "nah-muhs-KAA-ruh"
  • yān ≈ "yaan"
  • ninna pudar enchina? ≈ "NIN-nuh POO-dur EN-chi-nuh?"
  • enna pudar ≈ "EN-nuh POO-dur"
  • I ench ullar? ≈ "ee ench OOL-lur?"
  • kusald aande ≈ "koo-SULD AAN-deh"
  • daye malpule ≈ "DAH-yeh mahl-poo-leh"
  • mast upakāra ≈ "muhst oo-puh-KAA-ruh"

These are simple learner-friendly approximations for confidence, not exact phonetics.

Learning note

Tulu is often learned through romanized spellings online, and different sources may spell the same phrase a little differently.

For now, focus on saying the phrase smoothly and being understood. We can refine pronunciation as you go.

What to memorize first

  1. Namaskāra.
  2. Ninna pudar enchina?
  3. Enna pudar …
  4. Yān kusald aande.
  5. Mast upakāra.

Quiz 1: English to Tulu

  1. Hello.
  2. What is your name?
  3. My name is Maya.
  4. I am fine.
  5. Please help.

Quiz 2: Fill in the blank

  1. _____ (Hello)
  2. Ninna _____ enchina? (name)
  3. Enna _____ Ravi. (name)
  4. Yān _____ aande. (fine)
  5. Daye _____. (help / please help)

Quiz 3: Situations

Say the Tulu phrase you would use:

  1. You meet someone politely.
  2. You want to ask their name.
  3. You want to say your name is Alex.
  4. You want to say you are fine.
  5. You want to ask for help because your Tulu is weak.

Speaking drill

Say these aloud three times:

  1. Namaskāra.
  2. Ninna pudar enchina?
  3. Enna pudar Alex.
  4. Yān kusald aande.
  5. Daye malpule.
