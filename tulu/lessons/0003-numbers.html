Tulu • Lesson 3

Numbers

In this lesson, you will learn the most useful beginner Tulu numbers so you can count from 1 to 10 and use numbers in very simple everyday phrases.

Focus: counting, saying how many, and using small numbers in speech.

Practical objective

By the end, you should be able to count from 1 to 10, say a few simple quantities, and recognize common teen numbers like 11–15.

Core numbers: 1–10

onji = one
raDD = two
mUji = three
nAl = four
ain = five
Aji = six
El = seven
enma = eight
orumba = nine
patt = ten

Quick extensions: 11–15

  • pattonji — 11
  • padiraDD — 12
  • padimUji — 13
  • padinAl — 14
  • padinain — 15
  • You do not need to memorize all of these today — just notice the pattern.

Simple useful phrases with numbers

  • raDD kai — two hands
  • nAl ill — four houses
  • patt kilo ari — ten kilos of rice
  • enk raDD kai uNDu — I have two hands
  • yān raDD gaNTae kApuvae — I will wait for two hours

Pronunciation confidence hints

  • onji ≈ "ON-jee"
  • raDD ≈ "ruhd" or "rahdd"
  • mUji ≈ "MOO-jee"
  • nAl ≈ "naal"
  • ain ≈ "eye-n"
  • Aji ≈ "AA-jee"
  • El ≈ "ayl"
  • enma ≈ "EN-ma"
  • orumba ≈ "oh-RUM-buh"
  • patt ≈ "puhtt"

These are simple learner-friendly approximations for confidence, not exact phonetics.

Mini dialogue: counting together

Teacher: Onji, raDD, mUji...

You: nAl, ain, Aji...

Teacher: El, enma, orumba, patt.

You: Avu! = Okay!

Mini dialogue: using a number

Teacher: raDD kai.

You: raDD kai.

Teacher: enk raDD kai uNDu.

You: enk raDD kai uNDu.

This is a simple repeat-after-me speaking drill built from attested beginner phrases.

Pattern note

In beginner examples, the number usually comes before the noun.

Examples: raDD kai (two hands), nAl ill (four houses).

What to memorize first

Self-check answer key

  • one → onji
  • two → raDD
  • three → mUji
  • five → ain
  • seven → El
  • ten → patt
  • two hands → raDD kai
  • 1, 2, 3 → onji, raDD, mUji
  • 7, 8, 9, 10 → El, enma, orumba, patt
  • 14 → padinAl
  • ten kilos of rice → patt kilo ari
  • I have two hands → enk raDD kai uNDu
