# Portuguese lesson 3: Basic conversations

Goal: help the user hold a short, polite beginner conversation in Portuguese.

## What Portuguese is

Portuguese is a Romance language spoken in Portugal, Brazil, and several other countries. In this learning channel, the lessons are using **European Portuguese** as the main reference, so some vocabulary and pronunciation may differ from Brazilian Portuguese.

For a beginner, the most useful first goal is not perfect grammar. It is being able to:

- greet people
- introduce yourself
- ask simple questions
- understand common polite replies
- keep a conversation going for 30 to 60 seconds

## Core conversation phrases

These are strong beginner building blocks:

- **Olá.** = Hello.
- **Bom dia.** = Good morning.
- **Boa tarde.** = Good afternoon.
- **Boa noite.** = Good evening.
- **Tudo bem?** = How are you?
- **Tudo bem.** = I’m fine.
- **E consigo?** = And you? (formal)
- **E contigo?** = And you? (informal)
- **O meu nome é Quill.** = My name is Quill.
- **Chamo-me Quill.** = My name is Quill.
- **Sou dos Estados Unidos.** = I am from the United States.
- **Falo um pouco de português.** = I speak a little Portuguese.
- **Não percebo.** = I don’t understand.
- **Pode repetir, por favor?** = Can you repeat, please?
- **Fala inglês?** = Do you speak English?
- **Muito prazer.** = Nice to meet you.
- **Igualmente.** = Likewise / nice to meet you too.

## Simple question patterns

These help you keep a conversation moving:

- **Como se chama?** = What is your name? (formal)
- **Como te chamas?** = What’s your name? (informal)
- **De onde é?** = Where are you from? (formal)
- **De onde és?** = Where are you from? (informal)
- **Como está?** = How are you? (formal)
- **Como estás?** = How are you? (informal)

If you are unsure, beginner-safe defaults are:

- **Tudo bem?**
- **Como se chama?**
- **De onde é?**

## Useful answer patterns

- **Chamo-me Quill.** = My name is Quill.
- **Sou de Londres.** = I am from London.
- **Sou americano / americana.** = I am American.
- **Estou bem.** = I am well.
- **Mais ou menos.** = So-so.
- **Sim.** = Yes.
- **Não.** = No.

## Conversation repair phrases

These are very important for real communication:

- **Não percebo.** = I don’t understand.
- **Mais devagar, por favor.** = More slowly, please.
- **Pode repetir, por favor?** = Can you repeat, please?
- **Como se diz ... em português?** = How do you say ... in Portuguese?

These phrases let you stay in the conversation even when you miss something.

## Mini dialogue 1: meeting someone

**A:** Olá. Tudo bem?
**B:** Tudo bem. E consigo?
**A:** Tudo bem. O meu nome é Quill.
**B:** Muito prazer. Chamo-me Ana.
**A:** De onde é?
**B:** Sou do Porto.

## Mini dialogue 2: learner survival conversation

**A:** Olá. Fala inglês?
**B:** Um pouco.
**A:** Eu falo um pouco de português.
**B:** Muito bem!
**A:** Não percebo. Pode repetir, por favor?
**B:** Claro.

## Fast pronunciation help

- **tudo bem** ≈ "TOO-doo BANG"
- **consigo** ≈ "kohn-SEE-goo"
- **contigo** ≈ "kohn-TEE-goo"
- **percebo** ≈ "pair-SAY-boo"
- **repetir** ≈ "reh-peh-TEER"
- **prazer** ≈ "prah-ZEHR"

These are confidence approximations, not exact phonetics.

## What to memorize first

If the user learns only eight conversation phrases, learn these:

1. **Olá.**
2. **Tudo bem?**
3. **O meu nome é...**
4. **De onde é?**
5. **Falo um pouco de português.**
6. **Não percebo.**
7. **Pode repetir, por favor?**
8. **Muito prazer.**
