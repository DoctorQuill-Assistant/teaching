Portuguese • Lesson 4

Numbers, prices, time, and quantities

In this lesson, you will learn the most useful beginner Portuguese numbers for real life: counting, hearing prices, understanding simple times, and saying how many things you want.

Focus: practical European Portuguese numbers you can use immediately.

Ask follow-up questions any time. Your teacher can give more number drills, price practice, or extra pronunciation help.

What success looks like

  • Say numbers 0–10 with confidence.
  • Recognize common teen numbers and tens like 11, 12, 20, and 30.
  • Ask and understand simple prices.
  • Use numbers to order quantities and talk about time.

The key beginner patterns

Quanto custa? = How much does it cost?
São duas horas. = It is two o’clock.
Queria dois cafés. = I would like two coffees.

These are the three big real-life uses of numbers: money, time, and quantity.

Core numbers: 0–10

  • zero — zero
  • um / uma — one
  • dois / duas — two
  • três — three
  • quatro — four
  • cinco — five
  • seis — six
  • sete — seven
  • oito — eight
  • nove — nine
  • dez — ten

um / uma and dois / duas change with gender. As a beginner, just notice the common pairs.

Very useful extra numbers

11–15

  • onze — eleven
  • doze — twelve
  • treze — thirteen
  • catorze — fourteen
  • quinze — fifteen

Tens

  • vinte — twenty
  • trinta — thirty
  • quarenta — forty
  • cinquenta — fifty

You do not need all numbers at once. Start with 0–10, then 11, 12, 20, and 30.

Essential phrases with numbers

Quanto custa? = How much does it cost?
Custa cinco euros. = It costs five euros.
Queria dois cafés. = I would like two coffees.
Queria uma água. = I would like one water.
São três horas. = It is three o’clock.
Tenho quatro bilhetes. = I have four tickets.

Mini dialogue: buying something

You: Desculpe, quanto custa?

Seller: Custa três euros.

You: Queria duas, por favor.

Seller: Claro.

You: Obrigado.

Mini dialogue: simple time

You: Que horas são?

Local: São duas horas.

You: Obrigado.

Pronunciation confidence hints

  • um ≈ "oong"
  • dois ≈ "doysh"
  • três ≈ "treysh"
  • seis ≈ "saysh"
  • oito ≈ "OY-too"
  • quanto custa ≈ "KWAN-too KOOSH-tah"
  • vinte ≈ "VEEN-t"
  • trinta ≈ "TREEN-tah"

These are simple learner-friendly hints for confidence, not exact phonetics.

What to memorize first

  1. um / uma
  2. dois / duas
  3. três
  4. Quanto custa?
  5. Queria dois cafés.

Quiz 1: English to Portuguese

  1. one
  2. two
  3. three
  4. ten
  5. twenty

Quiz 2: Fill in the blank

  1. Custa _____ euros. (five)
  2. Queria _____ cafés. (two)
  3. São _____ horas. (three)
  4. Tenho _____ bilhetes. (four)
  5. _____ custa? (How much)

Quiz 3: Situations

Say the Portuguese phrase you would use:

  1. You want to ask the price of something.
  2. You want to say “I would like two coffees.”
  3. You want to say “It is two o’clock.”
  4. You want to say “three euros.”
  5. You want to say “one water.”

Speaking drill

Say these aloud three times:

  1. um, dois, três
  2. quatro, cinco, seis
  3. sete, oito, nove, dez
  4. Quanto custa?
  5. Queria dois cafés.

Self-check answer key

  • one → um or uma
  • two → dois or duas
  • three → três
  • ten → dez
  • twenty → vinte
  • It costs five euros. → Custa cinco euros.
  • I would like two coffees. → Queria dois cafés.
  • It is three o’clock. → São três horas.
