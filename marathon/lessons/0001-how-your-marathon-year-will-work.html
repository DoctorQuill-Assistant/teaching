Marathon Lesson 0001: How your marathon year will work

This first lesson gives you the big picture. The goal is not to train like a professional. The goal is to build enough consistency and durability to finish your marathon comfortably and confidently.

Your objective for today

Understand the 4 main parts of your marathon year and what each kind of run is supposed to do.

Consistency first. Specific marathon work later.

Your real goal

Your primary goal is simple: finish a marathon comfortably and healthy.

A nice pace goal can still exist in the background, but your best chance of getting a decent pace is by becoming durable first.

  • Primary goal: complete the marathon
  • Success style: finish comfortably
  • Useful mindset: train for durability, not ego

The 4 parts of your year

  1. Base phase — make 3 to 4 runs per week feel normal and sustainable.
  2. Endurance build — gradually stretch your long run and overall weekly tolerance.
  3. Marathon-specific phase — practice longer efforts, pacing discipline, and fueling.
  4. Taper — reduce fatigue and arrive fresh for race day.

You do not need to train hard all year. You need to train steadily all year.

Your main weekly ingredients

  • Easy run — builds aerobic fitness while keeping stress low.
  • Quality run — a controlled harder session like hills, steady work, or tempo-lite.
  • Long run — the key durability builder for marathon preparation.
  • Optional extra easy run — helpful when recovery is good.
  • Short home strength — keeps your body more resilient.

What your normal week may look like

Based on your schedule, a practical default week is:

  • Monday — easy run or short support run
  • Thursday — quality run
  • Friday — easy run or optional run
  • Sunday — long run

That structure is flexible. The goal is to fit training into real life, not pretend real life does not exist.

How hard should most running feel?

Most runs should feel easier than many runners think.

  • Easy runs: conversational, controlled, relaxed
  • Quality runs: purposeful, but not all-out
  • Long runs: patient early, steady late

If you train too hard too often, you do not become tougher. You usually just become tired, inconsistent, or injured.

What matters more than pace right now

  • showing up consistently
  • keeping easy days easy
  • gradually extending endurance
  • learning hydration and fueling before race day
  • staying healthy enough to train for months

Mini scenario

You miss one weekday run because work gets busy. What is the smart response?

  • Option A: cram two hard runs back to back
  • Option B: accept the missed run and continue the plan calmly

Best answer: Option B.

Marathon training rewards consistency over heroics.

  2. What kind of run should make up most of your training?

Simple answer key

  • Most training: easy running
  • When life is busy: steady consistency beats perfection

Your takeaway

You do not need a perfect year. You need a repeatable year.
