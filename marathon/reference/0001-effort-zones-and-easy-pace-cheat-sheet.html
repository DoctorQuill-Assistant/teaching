Marathon Reference 0001: Effort zones and easy pace cheat sheet

Quick review page for staying in the right effort most of the time so marathon training stays sustainable.

The big rule

Most runs should feel easy. If a run that is supposed to be easy starts feeling like a test, slow down. The goal is consistency, not proving fitness every day.

Easy most days Save hard for hard days Finish feeling in control

Simple effort scale

  • Easy effort — You can talk in full sentences. Breathing is controlled. You could keep going longer.
  • Moderate / steady effort — You can talk in short phrases, but not comfortably chat the whole time.
  • Hard effort — Talking is difficult. You are working, focused, and counting down the remaining time or distance.

How easy pace should feel

  • You should feel like you are holding back a little.
  • Your breathing should stay calm enough that speaking a full sentence is realistic.
  • If you use heart rate, easy running should generally feel clearly below your “working hard” zone.
  • If hills, heat, poor sleep, or stress make the same pace feel harder, slow down and protect the effort level.

Warning signs you are running too hard on an easy day

  • You keep wanting to check pace because you are trying to hit a number.
  • You cannot speak comfortably in full sentences.
  • Your breathing feels strained early in the run.
  • Your legs feel heavy for no clear reason.
  • You finish feeling drained instead of pleasantly worked.

What to do if a run feels too hard

  • Slow down immediately.
  • Ignore the watch pace for a few minutes and go by breathing and conversation feel.
  • Shorten the run a little if needed, especially if heat, fatigue, or stress are high.
  • Remember: adjusting is smart training, not failed training.

Quick self-check before and during a run

  • Before: Is today supposed to be easy, moderate, or hard?
  • During: Can I still speak in full sentences?
  • During: Am I running by ego or by plan?
  • After: Do I feel like I could have done a bit more?
