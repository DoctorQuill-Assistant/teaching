# Hessisch topic guide

## Lesson design preferences

Future Hessisch lessons in this topic should be optimized for the user, who wants short practical lessons with exercises, idioms, and active recall.

Each new lesson should include:
- a short practical objective
- core Hessisch phrases with standard German meaning
- simple pronunciation confidence hints for the most important words or phrases
- at least one mini dialogue
- a quiz section with recall or translation questions
- a small self-check or answer key
- encouragement to ask follow-up pronunciation questions
- a short note comparing Hessisch with standard German when helpful

## Pronunciation guidance style

Use lightweight learner-friendly approximations, not dense phonetics by default.

Preferred format:
- **babbele** ≈ "BAB-be-le"
- **net** ≈ "nett"

Keep pronunciation notes clearly marked as approximations for confidence, not exact phonetic transcription.

## Quiz preference

Bias lessons toward:
- translation from standard German to Hessisch
- fill-in-the-blank prompts
- mini response drills
- short situational prompts like "You greet a neighbor casually" or "You react with a funny local phrase"
