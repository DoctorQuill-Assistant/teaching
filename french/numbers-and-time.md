# French lesson 2: Numbers and time basics

Goal: help the user understand simple numbers and talk about time in everyday situations.

## Numbers 0 to 10

- **zéro** = 0
- **un** = 1
- **deux** = 2
- **trois** = 3
- **quatre** = 4
- **cinq** = 5
- **six** = 6
- **sept** = 7
- **huit** = 8
- **neuf** = 9
- **dix** = 10

## Useful higher numbers

- **onze** = 11
- **douze** = 12
- **treize** = 13
- **quatorze** = 14
- **quinze** = 15
- **seize** = 16
- **vingt** = 20
- **trente** = 30
- **quarante** = 40

## Ask and tell the time

- **Quelle heure est-il ?** = What time is it?
- **Il est deux heures.** = It is two o'clock.
- **Il est midi.** = It is noon.
- **Il est minuit.** = It is midnight.

Common patterns:

- **Il est une heure.** = It is one o'clock.
- **Il est trois heures et demie.** = It is three thirty.
- **Il est quatre heures moins le quart.** = It is three forty-five.

## Useful scheduling phrases

- **à huit heures** = at eight o'clock
- **ce soir** = this evening
- **demain** = tomorrow
- **aujourd'hui** = today
- **maintenant** = now

## Mini dialogue

**A :** Quelle heure est-il ?
**B :** Il est sept heures et demie.
**A :** Merci.
**B :** De rien.

## Pronunciation help

- **deux** ≈ "duh"
- **trois** ≈ "trwah"
- **huit** ≈ "weet"
- **heure** ≈ "uhr"
- **demie** ≈ "duh-mee"
