# French lesson 1: Greetings and introductions

Goal: help the user start a simple conversation politely in French.

## The most important rule

Say **bonjour** before asking for anything. In French, this matters a lot.

- **Bonjour.** = Hello / good day.
- **Bonsoir.** = Good evening.
- **Salut.** = Hi. (informal)

When leaving:

- **Bonne journée.** = Have a good day.
- **Bonne soirée.** = Have a good evening.
- **Au revoir.** = Goodbye.

## Introduce yourself

- **Je m'appelle Quill.** = My name is Quill.
- **Je suis américain / américaine.** = I am American.
- **Je parle un peu français.** = I speak a little French.
- **Je suis débutant / débutante.** = I am a beginner.

## Ask someone their name

- **Comment vous appelez-vous ?** = What is your name? (polite)
- **Tu t'appelles comment ?** = What's your name? (informal)

For beginner use, prefer **vous** forms with strangers.

## Polite social phrases

- **S'il vous plaît.** = Please.
- **Merci.** = Thank you.
- **Merci beaucoup.** = Thank you very much.
- **De rien.** = You're welcome.
- **Excusez-moi.** = Excuse me.
- **Pardon.** = Sorry / excuse me.

## Mini dialogue

**A :** Bonjour.
**B :** Bonjour.
**A :** Je m'appelle Quill. Et vous ?
**B :** Je m'appelle Marie.
**A :** Enchanté.
**B :** Enchantée.

## Pronunciation help

- **bonjour** ≈ "bohn-zhoor"
- **bonsoir** ≈ "bohn-swahr"
- **merci** ≈ "mehr-see"
- **au revoir** ≈ "oh ruh-vwahr"
