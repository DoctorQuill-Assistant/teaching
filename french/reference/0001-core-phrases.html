French Reference 0001: Core polite phrases

This quick reference supports the mission of ordering dinner politely in French.

Bonjour
IPA: /bɔ̃.ʒuʁ/ · Say it like: bohn-ZHOOR
Hello / good day. Use this first when starting an interaction.
Bonsoir
IPA: /bɔ̃.swaʁ/ · Say it like: bohn-SWAHR
Good evening. Often used from around 6pm onward.
S’il vous plaît
IPA: /sil vu plɛ/ · Say it like: seel voo pleh
Please.
Merci
IPA: /mɛʁ.si/ · Say it like: mehr-SEE
Thank you.
Je voudrais…
IPA: /ʒə vu.dʁɛ/ · Say it like: zhuh voo-DREH
I would like…
Je vais prendre…
IPA: /ʒə vɛ pʁɑ̃dʁ/ · Say it like: zhuh veh PRAHND-r
I’ll have…
L’addition, s’il vous plaît.
IPA: /la.di.sjɔ̃ sil vu plɛ/ · Say it like: la-dee-syohn seel voo pleh
The bill, please.

Pronunciation notes

French R
Usually made in the back of the throat, not like a strong English R.
Nasal vowels
In words like bon or the first part of bonjour, the vowel resonates through the nose. Do not fully pronounce an English N at the end.
Silent endings
Many final consonants in French are soft or silent, so listen for the vowel shape more than the spelling.
